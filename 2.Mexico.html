WELCOME TO MEXICO

AN IMPORTANT HISTORY:

The official name of Mexico is United Mexican States. The name is relatively new, since in 1821, after the independence of the Spanish empire, it was agreed to call the country the Mexican Empire. Three years later, in 1824, having as a paradigm the United States of America as the example of freedom and democracy, the constituents decided to adapt the name of the United States to the Mexican Republic. Mexico is located in the Pacific Ring of Fire. This causes a strong seismic and volcanic activity in the country. The border between the United States and Mexico is the second largest in the world, after which it divides Canada and the United States. Mexico City was built on the ruins of the Great Tenochtitlán (Aztec city) and on what was a huge lake. This has caused the city to gradually sink, having to constantly reinforce the architectural structures. Mexico City is one of the oldest in America. More than 20 million 843 thousand inhabitants live in Mexico City. This makes it, according to the UN, the fourth most populous city in the world. Above are Tokyo, the most populated in the world with 37 million 833 thousand inhabitants; Delhi, with 24 million 953 thousand inhabitants, and Shanghai with 22 million 991 thousand inhabitants.

ITINERARY

Day 1:THE ZOCALO:PLAZA DE LA CONSTITUCION

Normally known as Zócalo, it is the perfect place to delve into Mexican culture. With almost five centuries of history, this space is where you can see the convergence of the indigenous and viceregal past reflected in the architecture that surrounds this square. Surrounding the Zocalo you will find the Plaza de Santo Domingo where the Baroque style Church of Santo Domingo is located, where Pedro de Moctezuma, one of the sons of Moctezuma II is buried. Garibaldi Square: A night among Mariachis An excellent option is Plaza Garibaldi, famous for mariachi groups, romantic trios, music groups that meet with their typical costumes and instruments.With excellent typical gastronomy from Jalisco and the Valley of Mexico and indigenous music, you will spend a great veiled. A place very frequented by tourists and in which there is a great show of Mexican dances and traditions is the "Guadalajara by Night". Just 50 meters from the Garibaldi square.

Day 2: TEOTIHUACAN

These architectural remains, which during its heyday was the urban center with the highest population density, have been considered a World Heritage Site by UNESCO since 1987. Simply exceptional and a unique experience that we recommend you go to. Pyramid of the Sun The complex is made up of the Pyramid of the Sun, which can be seen from miles away and is the second largest pyramid in Mesoamerica. Crossing the road or causeway of the dead is the Pyramid of the Moon, with a square base of about 45 meters on each side is just smaller than the Pyramid of the Sun. In it is the statue of the Goddess of Agriculture.

Day 3:FRIDA KAHLO MUSEUM

"I will never, in all my life, forget your presence. You took me in pieces and gave me back whole, whole. " Frida Kahlo How can I not visit the house of an artist who felt as much and gave as much as Frida? A true icon of modern Mexican art. That is why visiting the Frida Kahlo house museum is a mandatory stop like eating tacos. Located in one of the most beautiful and oldest neighborhoods in Mexico City, the Casa Azul was converted into a museum in 1958, just a few years after the painter's death. Here are some of the most important works of the artist, such as: Viva la Vida (1954), Frida and the cesarean section (1931), Portrait of my father Wilhem Kahlo (1952), among others.

CUEMANCO XOCHIMILCO PIER

It is time to take a walk through the network of canals and what better than to do it in one of the typical colorful wooden boats called trajineras. During the walk you can see birds, the chinampas (the pre-Hispanic farming system) listen to mariachis, trios, marimba and taste more of the regional food. A unique moment of this trip, ideal also to make new friends.
